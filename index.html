The SHIELD Initiative

5 Million Families. $6 a Month. $500 Million in Pressure.

The Shield Initiative is a community-funded financial mechanism designed to make the insurance industry invest in ending gun violence against Black youth not through moral persuasion, but through math. By organizing 200,000 term life insurance policies in the highest-risk neighborhoods of Chicago and Philadelphia, the initiative creates $440 million in annual insurer exposure, more than double the entire industry's federal lobbying budget. The policies are owned by parents. Every dollar of every payout goes directly to the family. The cost to fund the tipping point: $6 per month from upper-income households. No government money. No new legislation. Just the same financial logic that gave us seatbelts and airbags pointed at the number one killer of Black youth in America.

Key Numbers

2,500–3,000 - Black youth killed by gun homicide every year.
$172M - Insurance industry's annual federal lobbying budget.
200,000 - Policies needed to cross the tipping point.
$440M - Annual insurer exposure at tipping point.
$6/month - Cost per contributing household.
5 million - Black households earning $100K+ who could fund it.
ZERO - The Shield Fund's financial interest in any death benefit.

How It Works

  • Families contribute a small monthly amount to the Shield Fund.
  • The fund pays life insurance premiums on behalf of youth in high-risk areas.
  • Concentrated policies create hundreds of millions in insurer exposure.
  • Insurers invest in violence prevention because it's cheaper than paying claims.
